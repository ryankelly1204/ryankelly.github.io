VEX Battery Box

A portable battery charging solution

Design Overview

In my senior year of high school, I had to complete a senior capstone project to finish my engineering concentration. Since I had a been a part of the VEX robotics team for four years, I recognized that our team had issues with battery transportation to and from competitions. Our existing battery carrier had many loose cables and wouldnâ€™t properly charge many of the batteries.

My solution was an entirely new battery box with 3D printed inserts that would perfectly fit the batteries and charge them while also ensuring the charging indicators would remain visible.

Modeling

Our high school had five robot teams and each one used two batteries, so I designed the holder in Autodesk Inventor to fit 10 batteries. Each battery charger is plugged into power strip inside the holder so only one cord needs to plug into the wall.

I used the battery files available from the VEX robotics website and designed the 3D printed holders to have a sliding fit so the batteries would drop onto a charger held in place on the bottom. Each charger was plugged into a 90 degree connector so the charging cable would point upward.

Manufacturing

With the CAD model complete, I created an Inventor drawing with the sizes of wood I needed to cut. I then cut the square pieces of wood on the table saw in our theater department, but had to manually sand the curves.

With all the pieces cut, I was able to assemble everything together.

Assembly

Because I had extensively designed the whole thing in CAD, the box fit together great! All of the 3D printed parts held the batteries perfectly, and the spacing between the wood was just right.
