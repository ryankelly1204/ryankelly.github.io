Shop Trainer Hammer

A machinst hammer for shop use

Overview

I am one of the prototyping shop trainers here at Northwestern. As a shop trainer, I train students on how to use both the basic and advanced machinery in the shop, including the mill and the lathe. In order to level up as a shop trainer and take independent shifts with lower level trainers shadowing you, you have to make a machinist hammer using the complex operations of both the mill and the lathe, including angling the head of the machine, external threading, and using a form tool.

Takeaways

This project took me several quarters to complete, but I am very happy with how the entire thing turned out. All of the parts are machined to a precision tolerance, and the two pieces screw together perfectly.
It was great to learn how to do complex operations on the mill and the lathe and now I feel more confident in my ability to teach new students how to use the machinery.
