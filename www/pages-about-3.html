PulseOx Pal

A biometric footwrap for infants

Design Overview

In my freshman year Design, Thinking, and Communication (DTC) class, students were placed in teams of four and paired with a client to solve a problem. My DTC group partnered with Nancy Cowles of Kids in Danger to find a way to prevent cases of Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS). In 10 weeks, my team focused on the physical aspects of the baby and how they could be monitored to try to prevent SIDS. We created a design of a biometric foot wrap and accompanying app that could keep track of a baby’s heart rate, blood oxygen levels, and position.

User Feedback

Our team started our design process researching what causes SIDS. The most common cause was asphyxiation that occurs when a baby’s airway closes. Then we conducted user interviews to gain feedback about four different prototypes, a bouncer that could convert to a flat basin, the biometric footwrap, a neck brace, and a neck pillow. The biometric foot wrap performed best in our testing, so we continued with it.

Prototyping

Due to the limitation of the class, we were unable to create a working sensor inside of the foot wrap. Instead we made a works-like and a looks-like model. Since I already had electronics experience from high school, I created the working sensor. I researched the different components I needed with the help of our mechatronics professor. We purchased an ESP32 Feather board, an IMU, and a pulse oximeter. Using some widely available resources online, I figured out how to wire the components together and created a webpage to display the live readings. Another teammate created the sewn model to demonstrate the ideal packaging of our design.

App Design

Another teammate designed the UI based off of the user feedback. He made a series of different pages within the app as well as example notifications for when the biometrics fall into dangerous territory.

Showcase

At the end of the quarter we presented our idea to Nancy Cowles at the DTC exposition. We created a poster displaying our idea and organized a final presentation. Kids in Danger didn’t move forward with our idea, but it was a great experience to be able to go through the whole design process and create a working prototype.
